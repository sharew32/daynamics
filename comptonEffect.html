Complete Derivation of the Compton Effect

1. Physical Setup

When a photon with initial wavelength λ collides with a stationary electron (rest mass me), two key phenomena occur:

  • The photon scatters at angle θ with increased wavelength λ'
  • The electron recoils at angle φ with momentum pe

This interaction demonstrates the particle nature of light while obeying conservation laws.

2. Derivation Using Relativistic Principles

Conservation of energy:

\[ h\nu + m_e c^2 = h\nu' + \sqrt{(p_e c)^2 + (m_e c^2)^2} \]

Conservation of momentum (x and y components):

\[ \frac{h\nu}{c} = \frac{h\nu'}{c} \cos \theta + p_e \cos \phi \]
\[ 0 = \frac{h\nu'}{c} \sin \theta - p_e \sin \phi \]

Combining energy and momentum equations and simplifying gives the Compton wavelength shift:

\[ \Delta \lambda = \lambda' - \lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c} (1 - \cos \theta) \]

Final Result: Compton Wavelength Shift

\[ \boxed{\Delta \lambda = \lambda' - \lambda = \frac{h}{m_e c} (1 - \cos \theta)} \]

Where:

  • \(\frac{h}{m_e c}\) = Compton wavelength of electron (≈ 0.00243 nm)
  • \(\theta\) = scattering angle of photon (0° to 180°)

Key Implications

  • Particle-Wave Duality: Direct evidence that photons carry momentum (\(p = h/λ\))
  • Relativistic Dynamics: Electron gains energy according to \(E_e' = \sqrt{(p_e c)^2 + (m_e c^2)^2}\)
  • Quantum Revolution: This 1923 experiment helped establish quantum mechanics
