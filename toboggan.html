Toboggan Acceleration Analysis


Given Data

Parameter Value
Path equation y = 0.01x² (parabolic curve)
Speed at point A (vA) 10 m/s
Tangential acceleration (at) 3 m/s² (increasing speed)

Schematic Diagram

[Toboggan moving along parabolic path y = 0.01x² at point A]

Step 1: Find Radius of Curvature (ρ)

For a curve y = f(x), the radius of curvature is:

ρ = [1 + (dy/dx)²]3/2 / |d²y/dx²|

First derivative:

dy/dx = 0.02x

Second derivative:

d²y/dx² = 0.02

At point A (x-coordinate unknown), we express ρ in terms of x:

ρ = [1 + (0.02x)²]3/2 / 0.02

Note: Without x-coordinate at point A, we cannot compute a numerical value for ρ.

Step 2: Calculate Normal Acceleration (an)

an = v²/ρ = 100/ρ m/s²

Requires ρ value from Step 1.

Step 3: Total Acceleration

Combining tangential and normal components:

a = √(at² + an²) = √(9 + (100/ρ)²) m/s²

Final Result

The magnitude of acceleration at point A is:

a = √[9 + (100/ρ)²] m/s²

where ρ = [1 + (0.02x)²]3/2 / 0.02

Component Expression
Tangential (at) 3.0 m/s²
Normal (an) 100/ρ m/s²
Total (a) √(9 + (100/ρ)²) m/s²

Note: The exact numerical value cannot be determined without the x-coordinate of point A on the parabola. The solution is presented in terms of the general position (x). If point A is at the vertex (x=0), then ρ = 50 m and a = 5 m/s².
