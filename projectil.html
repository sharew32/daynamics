Projectile Motion Analysis

Given Data

Parameter Value
Initial velocity (v₀) 120 m/s
Launch angle (θ) 40°
Horizontal distance to slope base 800 m
Slope angle 20°

Problem Schematic

[Projectile launched from point A at 40°, landing on 20° slope that begins 800m horizontally from A]

Step 1: Define the Coordinate System

Place point A at the origin (0, 0). The slope begins at x = 800 m and has the equation:

y = tan(20°) · (x - 800)

Where tan(20°) ≈ 0.3640

Step 2: Projectile Motion Equations

The projectile's position at time t is:

x(t) = v₀ cos(40°) · t = 120 × 0.7660 · t ≈ 91.92 t
y(t) = v₀ sin(40°) · t - ½ g t² = 120 × 0.6428 · t - 4.905 t² ≈ 77.14 t - 4.905 t²

Step 3: Solve for Time of Flight (T)

At impact (t = T), the projectile lands on the slope:

y(T) = tan(20°) · (x(T) - 800)

Substitute the motion equations:

77.14 T - 4.905 T² = 0.3640 × (91.92 T - 800)
77.14 T - 4.905 T² = 33.46 T - 291.2

Rearrange into quadratic form:

4.905 T² - 43.68 T - 291.2 = 0

Solve the quadratic equation (a = 4.905, b = -43.68, c = -291.2):

T = [43.68 ± √(1907.9 + 5712.2)] / 9.81
T = [43.68 ± 87.29] / 9.81

Taking the positive root:

T ≈ 13.35 s

Step 4: Find Slant Distance (s)

Calculate impact coordinates:

x(T) = 91.92 × 13.35 ≈ 1227.2 m
y(T) = 77.14 × 13.35 - 4.905 × (13.35)² ≈ 155.7 m

Horizontal distance from slope base:

Δx = 1227.2 - 800 = 427.2 m

Slant distance along slope:

s = Δx / cos(20°) ≈ 427.2 / 0.9397 ≈ 454.7 m

Alternatively using vertical component:

s = y(T) / sin(20°) ≈ 155.7 / 0.3420 ≈ 455.3 m

(The slight difference of ≈0.6m is due to rounding during calculations)

Final Results

Quantity Value
Time of flight (t) 13.35 seconds
Slant distance (s) 455 meters

Note: The solution correctly interprets the 800m as the horizontal distance to the slope's base, not the vertical drop. The projectile lands on the 20° slope after traveling approximately 1227.2m horizontally and 155.7m vertically from the launch point.
