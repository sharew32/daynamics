Step 1: Coordinate System Setup

We define our coordinate system with:

  • Reference line as x-axis (horizontal)
  • Car B moving along x-direction at y = 100m
  • Car A positioned at (70.71m, 70.71m) [100m at 45°]

Step 2: Velocity Analysis

Car A Velocity Components

Tangential velocity (direction of motion is downward to the right at 315°):

vA = 10cos315° î + 10sin315° ĵ = 7.07 î - 7.07 ĵ m/s

Car B Velocity Components

vB = 18.5 î + 0 ĵ m/s

Relative Velocity

vA/B = vA - vB = (7.07 - 18.5) î - 7.07 ĵ = -11.43 î - 7.07 ĵ m/s
|vA/B| = √[(-11.43)² + (-7.07)²] = 13.4 m/s

Step 3: Acceleration Analysis

Car A Acceleration Components

Tangential acceleration (in direction of motion at 315°):

aAt = 5cos315° î + 5sin315° ĵ = +3.535 î - 3.535 ĵ m/s²

Normal acceleration (toward center at 225°):

aAn = 1cos225° î + 1sin225° ĵ = -0.707 î - 0.707 ĵ m/s²

Total acceleration:

aA = (+3.535 î - 3.535 ĵ) + (-0.707 î - 0.707 ĵ) = +2.83 î - 4.24 ĵ m/s²
|aA| = √[(2.83)² + (-4.24)²] = 5.10 m/s²

Car B Acceleration Components

aB = 2 î + 0 ĵ m/s²

Relative Acceleration

aA/B = aA - aB = (2.83 - 2) î - 4.24 ĵ = +0.83 î - 4.24 ĵ m/s²
|aA/B| = √[(0.83)² + (-4.24)²] = 4.32 m/s²

Final Results

Quantity Value Components
Relative Velocity (vA/B) 13.4 m/s -11.43 î - 7.07 ĵ m/s
Relative Acceleration (aA/B) 4.32 m/s² +0.83 î - 4.24 ĵ m/s²

Note: This solution accounts for the correct direction of Car A's motion (downward to the right at 315°).The tangential acceleration has a positive x-component and negative y-component, while the normal acceleration points toward the center of curvature (225°). All values are calculated for the instant when t = 3s.
