Physics Problems Solutions

1. Crate Deceleration Problem

The 50-kg crate is projected along the floor with an initial speed of 7 m/s at \( x = 0 \). The coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.40. Calculate the time required for the crate to come to rest and the corresponding distance \( x \) traveled.

50 kg
v₀ = 7 m/s
μₖ = 0.40
x = 0

Step 1: Calculate Friction Force

\[ f_k = \mu_k \cdot N = \mu_k \cdot mg \]
\[ f_k = 0.40 \times 50 \times 9.81 = 196.2 \, \text{N} \]

Step 2: Determine Deceleration

\[ a = \frac{f_k}{m} = \frac{196.2}{50} = -3.924 \, \text{m/s}^2 \]

Step 3: Calculate Time to Stop

\[ v = v_0 + at \]
\[ 0 = 7 - 3.924t \]
\[ t = \frac{7}{3.924} \approx 1.78 \, \text{s} \]

Step 4: Calculate Distance Traveled

\[ x = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \]
\[ x = 7 \times 1.78 + \frac{1}{2}(-3.924)(1.78)^2 \]
\[ x \approx 6.24 \, \text{m} \]

Final Answers:

Time to stop: \( \boxed{1.78 \, \text{s}} \)

Distance traveled: \( \boxed{6.24 \, \text{m}} \)

2. Spring Work Problem

The spring is unstretched when \( x = 0 \). If the body moves from the initial position \( x_1 = 100 \, \text{mm} \) to the final position \( x_2 = 200 \, \text{mm} \), (a) determine the work done by the spring on the body and (b) determine the work done on the body by its weight.

7 kg
x₁ = 100 mm
x₂ = 200 mm
k = 4 kN/m
20°

Part (a): Work Done by Spring

\[ U_{\text{spring}} = \frac{1}{2}k(x_1^2 - x_2^2) \]
\[ U_{\text{spring}} = \frac{1}{2}(4000)(0.1^2 - 0.2^2) \]
\[ U_{\text{spring}} = 2000(0.01 - 0.04) = -60 \, \text{J} \]

Part (b): Work Done by Weight

\[ \Delta x = x_2 - x_1 = 0.1 \, \text{m} \]
\[ \Delta y = \Delta x \sin\theta = 0.1 \sin(20°) \approx 0.0342 \, \text{m} \]
\[ U_{\text{weight}} = mg\Delta y = 7 \times 9.81 \times 0.0342 \approx 2.35 \, \text{J} \]

Final Answers:

(a) Spring work: \( \boxed{-60 \, \text{J}} \)

(b) Weight work: \( \boxed{2.35 \, \text{J}} \)
